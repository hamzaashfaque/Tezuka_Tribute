Anime: How it all began

Definition

Anime is simply just the japanese term for animation. Most people use the term to decribe specific type of animations with colorful 2D graphics.

Propaganda and other Early Animations

In 1917, Animators like Oten Shimokawa and Seitorou Kitayama started creating the first Japanese animations. However, some of these early animations and clips were destroyed in the 1923 due to an earthquake. In 1930s and 1940s Japan would start producing propaganda animations in support of Japanese government due to the ongoing World War II conflicts between Japan and it's enemies China and other fractured Chinese States, The Soviet Union and The United States. These animations were aimed mostly at Japanese children and were also sponsored by the Imperial Government.

A good example is Momotaro no Umiwashi which was aired in 1943. Another one is Momotaro's Sacred Sailors which was aired in 1945. Both of these directed by Mitsuyo Seo and were Sponsored by the Imperial Japanese Navy.

Osamu Tezuka

After the war in 1950s, many studios and animators would start adapting techniques of American animations to create their own cheaper mangas and animations like Otogi Manga Calander, Three Tales, Mole's Adventure.

Osamu Tezuka also known as The Father of Manga, expeimented with and adapted many animation techniques used by American Animations. His efforts lead to the growing of animation industries in Japan due to new better quality and cost effective animation techniques. His works include Astro Boy, Kimba, Princess Knight, Pheonix, Black Jack, Dororo and many more. His works were influenced by Disney. He also met with Walt Disney personally in the 1964 New York World's Fair. Tezuka was also the descendant of the Ninja Hattori Hanzo who served Shogun Tokugawa Ieyasu.

First Anime Series and Films

The Tale of the White Serpant, an animation based on a Chinese legend was aired in 1958 by TOEI animations. It could be considered the first anime compared to the standards of modern style animations. The animation however, was not able make enough profits.

In 1963, Astro Boy was aired on NTV as a TV series produced by Mushi Production. It was directed by Osamu Tezuka. Many consider it to be the first popular anime TV series. About 193 episodes were aired, with the final being aired on 1966. Production of Astro Boy lead to innovation in better quality and cost effective animation techniques.

In 1965, Kimba the White Lion was aired on Fuji Television and was considered the first colour TV series. It was also written by Osamu Tezuka.
